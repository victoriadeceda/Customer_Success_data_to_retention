This project showcases a Customer Success Manager assessment for Beyond Pricing. It includes a customer-facing presentation on Health Score and Base Price, an executive business review focused on renewal and ROI, and a sample email response. The goal was to demonstrate product education, strategic account management and analytical thinking.

## Teaching core concepts

Context: a new customer that's only been using the tool for a few months without making adjustments to fully exploit Beyond's value. 

Before diving into key strategic concepts, I've asked some discovery questions:

''*I noticed you haven’t made adjustments recently which is totally normal early on but it usually means there’s something unclear.*''
- Friction - Did the client not have time to review or being overwhelmed and not knowing what to adjust? 
- Health Score - Is it clear or created more confusion?
- Base price - How do you feel regarding your pricing? 
- How often are you reviewing your pricing and performance? 
- What triggers you to make a change in your price?

### Health Score

It shows how your listing is performing compared to demand in your market. It helps you understand if bookings are to slow (prices may be too high), to quickly (prices may be to low), or just right. It’s based on the market trends, your listing’s performance compared to other listings and your booking pace. 

#### Benefits
We want to highlight the value the customer will get by applying these changes. 
- Boost your revenue: it makes sure you’re not leaving money on the table but also it helps you avoid empty nights. 
- Better pricing decisions: it helps you decide when you need to make an adjustment (based on real data) instead of guessing. 
- Keep your listing competitive: as the demand and seasonality change, your prices too.
- Boost your listing: making constant adjustments based on real data provided by Beyond, you’re maximizing the potential of your listings. 

#### Next steps
Action points will give the customer a clear picture of what to do next. 
- Check your Health Score regularly: ideally weekly or biweekly. 
- If you’re booking too fast (High score): increase your base price to get more revenue from the high demand. 
- If you’re booking to slow (Low score): lower your base price to improve occupancy and visibility during low demand.

### Base Price

It’s your listing’s average nightly price across the year. It’s the starting point for all price fluctuations. From there, Beyond automatically adjusts based on demand: seasonality, day of the week, availability, local events, etc. Meaning that prices will go above your base price during high demand and low during low demand. 

#### Benefits
- Big impact on your revenue: if you have a base price too low, you may be leaving money on the table, and if it’s too high, you may miss bookings. 
- Dynamic pricing: it keeps in mind the demand (all aspects mentioned above) for you to maximize your revenue. 
- Stay aligned with your market: it takes data from the real world around your listing to make better decisions.
- Reduce your work: by setting the base price, Beyond will adjust automatically, meaning you don’t need to manually edit the price every night/week. 

#### Next steps
- Review Comp Sets tab: check other listings with good health scores. Do not copy their prices. Instead, ask yourself ‘’What makes my listing unique?’’ → then you may be able to set a higher price. 
- Neighborhood Pricing Chart: compare Airbnb and Vrbo pricing to see if there’s alignment with Beyond’s Base price helper suggestion.

We'll take this opportunity to make changes now and set a clear next step: 
''*Let’s adjust this together today and check back in a couple of weeks, then we should start seeing a booking pattern based on the changes we make today.*''

## Renewal Strategy 

Context: vacation rental company with 40 properties who are up for renewal and have asked for a 10% discount (Beyond charges 1% on all bookings). 

### Performance review - Revenue & Market comparison
We want to highlight the profit margin that they got after joining Beyond. 

- €440K+ total revenue vs €379k (2023 revenue - pre-Beyond): that's a 60k profit from last year's. 
- Outperform the market: Beyond Dynamic pricing captured the high demand effectively! 
  - July: 99% occupancy vs 85% market
  - August: 97% vs 75%
  - September: 82% vs 24% 
- Peak performance during summer months: high occupancy, strong booking demand, longer stays and increased lead time.
- Healthy booking behaviour
  - Lead time increases up to 100+ days in peak season
  - Average stay increases to 7 nights
- Insights: Guests booking earlier and staying longer

#### Discussing ROI and discount request
Strong ROI
- Revenue: +€440K 
- Investment: €4.4K (1%)

''*So looking at the full year, you’ve generated roughly €440K in revenue. Your investment in Beyond is about €4,400. That’s a very small percentage relative to the performance gains we’re seeing*''

When customers push back, we need to be prepared: 
''*Our goal is to make the 1% feel insignificant compared to the revenue we’re driving and in this case, I believe it is.*'' We need to be firm about our decision.  

Base your response in facts: ''*If we reduce investment but lose even a small percentage of optimization, that could have a much larger impact on revenue than the cost itself.*'' 

As well as on value: ''*I’d love to focus on increasing your revenue even further especially in low-performing months and take this momentum to keep growing.*'' 

I received the following push back: 
''Yes but, we're still not sure. We're actually thinking of hiring a revenue manager to do this all for us''. 
We need to think fast and do the math: 
- How much would a person cost? Approx 40k per year while Beyond's investment is 4.4k.
- Fight back: ''*Totally understandable. This is exactly what Beyond is for, we manage the revenue for you. If you were to hire someone your investment will jump to 40k per year while Beyond is costing you 4.4k. Then the 60k profit you made will definetly be impacted by this*''

While this year’s night rate is lower (€200) than last year’s (€250), it’s also important to look at performance to better understand pricing. That being said, if this rate is below your requirements, I can suggest to set a higher minimum price to avoid this.
